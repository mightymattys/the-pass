## Acceptance Evidence

- Canonical `Instrument` and `CanonicalEvent` contracts use `Decimal`, UTC nanoseconds,
  explicit event/receive time, deterministic ordering, and SHA-256 raw fingerprints.
- Immutable Parquet partitions use `source/venue/instrument/date`, sibling staging, atomic
  rename, and refusal to replace an existing partition.
- DuckDB queries Parquet in memory and is not a source of truth.
- Quality reports cover missing intervals, duplicates, timestamp disorder, sequence gaps,
  invalid values, crossed/stale books, outliers, timezone, session/roll gaps, provider
  truncation, and negative receive latency.
- Binance and Polymarket adapters use only public read-only data. Futures uses a synthetic
  Databento-compatible fixture and stays diagnostic without a licensed archive.
- REST and WebSocket infrastructure records fingerprints, provider failures, reconnects,
  heartbeat configuration, and immutable raw payloads.
- Polymarket fees are token-specific and timestamped. There is no flat fee constant.
- Polymarket book sequence/hash mismatches require snapshot resync.
- Futures raw contracts remain separate; the reference volume roll emits a back-adjusted
  signal price and a concrete execution contract.
